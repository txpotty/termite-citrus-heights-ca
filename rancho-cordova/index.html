Recognizing the Need for Termite Control in Rancho Cordova, CA

Don't delay; early detection and treatment are key to saving your property from costly damage.

Common Signs of Termite Infestation

Several signs can indicate the presence of termites. One of the most visible signs of subterranean termites is the presence of mud tubes. These earthen tunnels, often found on foundation walls, floor joists, or other structural elements, serve as protected pathways for termites as they travel between their underground colony and their food source (the wood in your home). Finding these tubes is a strong indicator of termite activity and warrants immediate investigation. Another common sign, particularly during warmer months, is the appearance of termite swarmers. These are winged reproductive termites that emerge from established colonies to mate and start new ones. Discarded wings, often found near windowsills, doors, or light fixtures, are a clear sign that a termite colony is nearby and has been active. Wood damage can also be a strong indicator, though it may not always be outwardly visible. Termites eat wood from the inside out, so damaged areas may appear normal on the surface but sound hollow when tapped. Bubbling or peeling paint, or areas that appear water-damaged, can also be signs of termites tunneling beneath the surface. Small piles of what looks like sawdust, known as frass, are indicative of drywood termites. These are the fecal pellets of the termites and are often found near infested wood. If you notice any of these signs, it's time to call in the experts for a professional termite inspection in Rancho Cordova, CA. Ignoring these warning signs can lead to extensive and expensive damage to your property. We strongly recommend scheduling an inspection at the first hint of any of these indicators to prevent the problem from escalating.

Hollow-Sounding Wood

Termites consume wood from the inside, creating hollowed-out galleries within beams, walls, or other wooden structures. As a result, affected wood may sound hollow when tapped or knocked upon. This is because the inner structure has been compromised by the termites' feeding activity. This can be a subtle sign, early on, but can become more pronounced as the infestation progresses. If you notice that a wooden beam or structural element sounds different when tapped compared to other areas, it could be a sign of internal termite damage. This hidden damage is one of the reasons termites are often called 'silent destroyers.' By the time the damage is outwardly visible, the infestation may be quite advanced. A professional inspection can utilize specialized tools, like sounding hammers or infrared cameras, to detect these internal voids and confirm the presence of termites, even when there are no visible external signs of damage. Don't assume that just because the wood looks normal on the outside it is free from termites; it's the internal damage that is the real concern. If you are suspicious about the integrity of any wooden elements in your home or business in Rancho Cordova, CA, it's worth having them professionally inspected for termite activity, as hollow-sounding wood is rarely a good sign when it comes to structural components.
